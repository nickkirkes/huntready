- E02 S02.6: Geometry overlay fixture — added `ingestion/states/montana/build_overlay_fixture.py` and shared `ingestion/ingestion/lib/overlays.py` (`OverlayFixtureRow` + `DroppedOverlayPair` + `ROLE_FOR_E03_BY_CHILD_KIND` TypedDicts/mapping for E03 import). Output: `ingestion/states/montana/fixtures/geometry-overlays.json` (kept) and `geometry-overlays-dropped.json` (audit log) — both deterministic, sorted, paired-atomic write. **Architectural shift confirmed during implementation:** the original spec's PostGIS cross-join SQL approach hit Supabase's role-locked 2-min `statement_timeout` on real MT data (~12k candidate pairs × ~113 KB MultiPolygons; per-row geometry detoasting dominated even with `geometry_geom_gix` reachable). Implementation moved spatial work off SQL entirely to local shapely + STRtree against a single bulk `SELECT id, kind, ST_AsText(geom) FROM geometry WHERE state = 'US-MT'` — total ~5 sec end-to-end for 349 geometries / ~26k candidate pairs across all relationship types. Three-band area-ratio discriminator (codified as **ADR-016**, accepted) replaced binary `ST_Covers`: `overlap_pct = parent.intersection(child).area / child.area`; `>=0.99 → "covers"` (digitization-tolerant), `<0.01 → drop` (boundary-touching artifact, written to audit log), otherwise `"intersects"` (genuine partial overlap). Threshold calibration is empirical to MT data; `child.area` denominator is asymmetric and serves the consumer's question ("what HDs is this child a member of?"). Pre-fix UAT showed 411 portion `intersects` rows of which 262 (<0.1% overlap) were boundary-touching noise and 102 (≥99%) were de facto covers rejected by strict `ST_Covers` — only 23 of 55 portions strictly contained. Post-fix: ~390 fixture rows (was 967), all 55 portions get ≥1 `covers`, epic invariant satisfied. Per-ID `EXPECTED_RA_ORPHAN_IDS` allowlist (Glacier NP, Sun River Game Preserve, Yellowstone NP) tolerates 3 documented no-hunt zones with code-edit + human-review gate; first implementation tolerated all RA orphans at the kind level and was rejected as too coarse during review. Two E03 handoff items recorded in the epic: (a) `kind='restricted_area'` conflates internal HD restrictions with no-hunt zones — E03 will likely need a discriminator subtype or a multi-bind role mechanic; (b) jurisdiction_binding fan-out is higher than naive estimates — median ~3 parent HDs per child after threshold filtering, with one RA tied to 16 parents. Threshold-edge tests (`overlap_pct = 0.989/0.990/0.011/0.009`) lock in the contract. Ruff/mypy/cubic clean; PR #25 merged 2026-05-01
