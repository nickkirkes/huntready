## Phase A — column-layout diagnosis

Page 73 (0-indexed page 72; page size **603 × 783** pt) lays the mandatory-inspection rule out as a **two-narrow-column flow under one wide heading**, with a structurally separate right-column section:

| Region | bbox `(x0, top, x1, bottom)` | Content |
|--------|------------------------------|---------|
| Heading (wide) | `(0.0, 36.0, 310.0, 52.0)` | `"Mandatory Bear Inspections & Seals"` |
| Col A body | `(14.0, 52.0, 162.0, 226.0)` | "Hunters must personally present…" → ends "…the jaw is propped" (table heading "Limited Licenses" begins at top≈228) |
| Col B body | `(165.0, 52.0, 312.0, 284.0)` | "open with a stick…" → ends "…prepared for human consumption." (table header "Unit Valid GMUs…" begins at top≈286) |
| Right column (excluded) | x ≥ 324 | `"Multiple Options for Hunting Bear"` + Hunter A/B prose |

**Root cause:** the prior code called raw pdfplumber `page.extract_text()` on the **un-cropped** page, which interleaves the columns left-to-right line-by-line. The END-anchor `_BEAR_INSPECTION_END_RE` ("Multiple Options for Hunting Bear") is the **right-column heading** (x≈324, same top=38 as the rule heading), so it appeared in the interleaved stream immediately after the rule heading and truncated `verbatim_rule` to just `"Mandatory Bear Inspections & Seals"`. Same failure class as S06.5's multi-column NPS/AFA split.

**The fix** crops each region separately so column text is read in faithful order (**heading → Col A → Col B**) and the right column is excluded by geometry. Col A's last sentence flows into Col B ("…the jaw is propped" + "open with a stick before rigor mortis sets in."), which the heading→A→B assembly preserves. The existing `re.sub(r"\s+", " ", …).strip()` normalization is unchanged, so soft-hyphens at line wraps are preserved verbatim per ADR-008 (e.g. "inspec- tion", "Colo- rado", "342- 7430", "por- tions"). A fail-loud guard (heading crop must contain the START anchor; both columns must be non-empty) raises (via `_build_base_records`) if a future brochure edition shifts these regions, rather than silently re-truncating.

## New `verbatim_rule` (full p.73 prose, 1238 chars)

> Mandatory Bear Inspections & Seals Hunters must personally present their bear head and hide to any CPW office (see inside front cover) during normal business hours, or by appointment with a CPW officer, for a free inspection, check report and sealing within five working days after harvest. Bear heads and hides must be unfrozen when presented for inspec- tion. Seals must be attached to the hide until tanned. At inspection, CPW is authorized to extract and keep a premolar tooth. If the head and hide are frozen, CPW may keep them long enough to thaw so that a tooth can be removed. Hunters can help by making sure the jaw is propped open with a stick before rigor mortis sets in. Bears cannot be taken out of Colo- rado until head and hide are inspected and sealed. Having a bear hide without a seal after the five-day period is illegal, and the hide becomes state property. To transport a bear or parts to a foreign country, you must first obtain CITES documents. Contact the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, 303-342- 7430. Do not call the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service about inspections or seals for bears. Meat does not need to be presented at the check, however all edible por- tions of meat must be prepared for human consumption.
